About Top Call Tracking

About Olivia Collins and the editorial standards behind the top call tracking rankings: how we test attribution accuracy, ease of use, integrations, and value for money.

About Olivia Collins

Olivia Collins has spent nine years editing software buyer's guides, most of it in the marketing-technology space where the gap between a vendor's pitch and a buyer's reality is widest. She started Top Call Tracking to do one thing properly: rank call tracking platforms the way an honest editor would, on a published rubric, with the testing to back it up.

The category needed it. Call tracking reviews online tend to fall into two camps: thin affiliate pages that rank whoever pays the most, and vendor blogs that rank themselves. Neither helps a marketer trying to choose. This site sits in the middle, with a single editor's name on the verdict and a method anyone can audit.

How this site works

Top Call Tracking reviews call tracking software and ranks it. We set up each platform, run real calls through it, and score it on four dimensions weighted equally: attribution accuracy, ease of use, integrations, and value for money. We publish the scores and the reasoning. We do not run a platform of our own and we do not sell call tracking. We are an independent editorial team that reviews software.

The site is reader-supported. When you sign up for a platform through one of our links, we may earn a referral commission, at no extra cost to you. That commission does not change the ranking. The rubric is public on the methodology page, and we apply it the same way to every platform, including the one we rank first.

Who this site is for

This site is for marketers, agencies, and business owners choosing a call tracking tool. It is not a general martech blog. The framing cares about whether the attribution is accurate, whether a normal person can set the thing up, whether it connects to your stack, and whether the price holds up as you grow. Those are the questions a buyer actually asks, so those are the questions we answer.

Editorial standards

Every platform reviewed here was set up and tested, not summarized from a brochure. Pricing is checked against vendor sites at the time of writing. When a platform ships a release that moves its score, we update the review and the date. Our top pick today is CallScaler, and the reasons are documented in its review and in the scorecard on every page.

How this site makes money

We earn affiliate fees on links to CallScaler. We are independent and are not owned by, or owners of, any platform reviewed. The fee does not buy a ranking. If a competitor outscored CallScaler on our rubric, it would take the cover and we would say so plainly.

Get in touch

For corrections, vendor updates, or methodology questions, the contact page has our email. We reply to methodology notes within two business days.
